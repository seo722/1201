NAVIGATION
To date, astronomers have identified more than 3,900 “exoplanets” –worlds orbiting stars other than the sun. there’s a “hot Saturn” 260 light-years from Earth that orbits its planet star so rapidly that a year there lasts less than three days. Circling another star 150 light-years out is a “hot Jupiter,” whose upper atmosphere is being blown away by the star’s solar winds. Astronomers have also found five planets orbiting a pulsar—the remains of a once mighty star shrunk into an atomic city-size nucleus that spins. Some worlds have fallen into their suns. Others have been thrown out of their systems to become “floaters” that wander in eternal darkness.
